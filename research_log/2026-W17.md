## 🥇 Paper 1 — PRIORITY ★★★

**Learning Versatile Humanoid Manipulation with Touch Dreaming**
[arXiv:2604.13015](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13015)
Yaru Niu et al. — Carnegie Mellon University. arXiv 14 Apr 2026.

### 🎯 (a) Q# / H# touched

Q5 (visuotactile encoder design) — direct hit. H6 (contact-consistency loss produces object-agnostic policies) — directly challenged/extended: the contribution here is a *predictive* contact loss rather than a reconstruction-consistency loss, which may be a stronger signal.

### ✨ (b) What is genuinely new

Prior visuotactile BC methods consume tactile as an observation modality but do not train the Transformer trunk to anticipate contact. HTD (Humanoid Transformer with Touch Dreaming) adds two auxiliary prediction heads trained jointly with behavioral cloning: (1) prediction of future hand-joint force magnitudes, and (2) prediction of future tactile *latents* (not raw tactile images). The critical ablation result is that latent-space prediction outperforms raw-pixel tactile prediction by **30% relative improvement** in success rate across 5 tasks. This reframes the role of tactile in the encoder: not as a sensor to decode, but as a supervisory signal shaping the shared trunk's contact-aware representations. The policy achieves a **90.9% relative improvement** over the stronger BC baseline on the 5 real-world contact-rich tasks (Insert-T, Book Organization, Towel Folding, Cat Litter Scooping, Tea Serving). No sim-to-real transfer — all training is from real-robot VR demonstrations augmented by the touch-dreaming prediction objective.

### ⚙️ (c) Decision implication

If the 30% latent-vs-raw gap holds for the Deform Map modality: **do not feed raw Deform Map frames directly into the policy encoder; instead use a learned latent compressor and add a future-latent prediction auxiliary loss.** Concrete pipeline change: add a `TactileDreamer` auxiliary head to the policy network that predicts `latent_t+k` from `obs_t` during training. Set prediction horizon `k ∈ {1, 3}` steps as a sweep. Training loss weight for the dreaming head should start at `λ_dream = 0.1` (ablate vs. 0.05, 0.2). This does not require additional real-robot data — it is a loss-only modification. Expected payoff: sharper contact-onset representations in the encoder, reducing the `observation_choice` bottleneck identified in Q2.

### ⚠️ (d) Failure mode to probe first

HTD is an IL system trained on real-robot demonstrations — it never sees sim. The "latent dreaming" benefit may be driven by the large real-robot dataset (multi-task VR demo corpus) rather than the loss design alone. In a sim-trained PPO pipeline with 8,192–16,384 envs, the contact-signal distribution from Deform Map sim will differ structurally from the real Deform Map signals the dreaming head would need to generalize over. The auxiliary head may overfit to simulated contact artifacts (PhysX point-contact) that do not match real Deform Map deformation fields, potentially *hurting* transfer rather than helping. **Prerequisite experiment:** train a small (500 real rollout) probing classifier on frozen encoder representations — does the dreaming-augmented encoder produce more linearly separable contact-type embeddings than a raw-tactile BC encoder on real data?

## 🥈 Paper 2 — PRIORITY ★★

**Grounding Sim-to-Real Generalization in Dexterous Manipulation: An Empirical Study with Vision-Language-Action Models**
[arXiv:2603.22876](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22876)
Ruixing Jin et al. — Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen. arXiv 24 Mar 2026.

### 🎯 (a) Q# / H# touched

Q3 (Sim2Real quantification — direct hit on sub-question (b): which DR dimensions actually matter). H3 (RMA extrinsics low-dimensional subspace) — indirectly: their factorial study quantifies which physical dimensions dominate the sim2real gap, which overlaps with what RMA extrinsics need to capture.

### ✨ (b) What is genuinely new

This is the first paper to run a controlled **factorial ablation across four sim2real dimensions** — (1) multi-level domain randomization, (2) photorealistic rendering, (3) physics-realistic modeling, and (4) reinforcement fine-tuning (RFT/online RL updates) — all evaluated on a unified **VLA model** with **10,000 real-world trials**. The key finding is that the four dimensions interact non-linearly: photorealistic rendering alone is insufficient without physics-realistic modeling, but their combination plus DR narrows the gap substantially. RFT provides a further improvement but exhibits diminishing returns once the sim-environment is already well-calibrated. They release both the robotic platform specification and the evaluation protocol for independent replication.

### ⚙️ (c) Decision implication

The evaluation protocol they release is directly adoptable for the Q3 sim2real protocol gap (see Section 10 of `research_context.md`). **Concrete action:** adopt their 4-dimension factorial design as the backbone for the Sharpa sim2real evaluation protocol, using their object variation axes (background, lighting, distractor, object type, spatial features) as the standard conditions. For the Isaac Lab pipeline, this means adding a "sim calibration score" checkpoint before any real-robot deployment: run the 4-dimension ablation on a fixed single task (e.g., in-hand cube rotation) before scaling to new objects. Expected overhead: ~200 real-world trials per ablation cell.

### ⚠️ (d) Failure mode to probe first

The study uses a VLA backbone (not a pure RL policy) and a parallel-jaw humanoid arm — not a 22-DOF fingertip-contact hand. Friction-randomization on fingertip contacts may not behave the same as on flat pads; their DR ranges for surface friction may need upward revision for 22-DOF tendons. Their "physics-realistic modeling" dimension is instantiated via contact parameter tuning, not PhysX Signorini-gap correction — so their findings on physics fidelity may not directly map to the Isaac Lab PhysX contact model gap for Sharpa's soft fingertips. The 10k trial evaluation is also on a simpler task set than in-hand reorientation.

## 🥉 Paper 3 — PRIORITY ★

**Tac2Real: Reliable and GPU Visuotactile Simulation for Online Reinforcement Learning and Zero-Shot Real-World Deployment**
[arXiv:2603.28475](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28475)
Ningyu Yan et al. — Shanghai AI Laboratory / HKUST. arXiv 30 Mar 2026.

### 🎯 (a) Q# / H# touched

Q5 (visuotactile encoder design — specifically the simulation infrastructure that would enable training with Deform Map feedback in Isaac Lab). Q3 (sim2real gap — tactile channel). H6 (contact-consistency loss requires a tactile sim faithful enough to train on).

### ✨ (b) What is genuinely new

Existing tactile simulators face a binary: fast-but-inaccurate (penalty-based, e.g., TacSL) or accurate-but-offline (FEM/GIPC, e.g., TacEx). Tac2Real threads the needle by integrating **PNCG-IPC** (Preconditioned Nonlinear Conjugate Gradient Incremental Potential Contact), a high-fidelity contact mechanics solver, into a **multi-node, multi-GPU parallel architecture** that achieves interactive-rate marker displacement field generation. This makes online RL with realistic tactile feedback feasible for the first time at training throughput comparable to standard PhysX-only policies. The companion **TacAlign** method explicitly narrows both structured domain gap (sensor geometry, elastomer stiffness) and stochastic domain gap (marker noise statistics), enabling zero-shot transfer on peg insertion.

### ⚙️ (c) Decision implication

If Tac2Real's zero-shot peg-insertion transfer is reproducible, it provides a path to adding Deform Map simulation into the Isaac Lab training loop without abandoning GPU-parallel RL. **Concrete action:** evaluate Tac2Real's TacAlign calibration procedure on the Sharpa Deform Map sensor by comparing simulated vs. real marker displacement statistics on 3 fixed contact poses. If KL divergence between sim and real displacement distributions is below the threshold they report for peg insertion (check their paper's Table 2), then visuotactile RL with Deform Map feedback becomes a near-term experiment rather than a future research direction. If not, use the structured gap finding to update `env_cfg.py` stiffness and damping parameters.

### ⚠️ (d) Failure mode to probe first

Tac2Real is demonstrated only on peg insertion with a parallel-jaw gripper — single contact patch, controlled geometry. Sharpa Deform Map operates on 10 independent fingertip sensors with spatially coupled contacts during in-hand manipulation. Multi-finger contact produces correlated marker displacement fields that may not be well-modeled by independent-sensor PNCG-IPC instances. The TacAlign structured gap correction assumes a single elastomer model per sensor — tendon coupling in Sharpa creates force cross-talk between fingers that is not captured by any current tactile sim. **The multi-finger correlated contact case is unvalidated.**

## 🌱 Paper 4 — CROSS-POLLINATION (adjacent field)

**A-SLIP: Acoustic Sensing for Continuous In-Hand Slip Estimation**
[arXiv:2604.08528](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08528)
Uksang Yoo, Yuemin Mao, Jean Oh, Jeffrey Ichnowski — Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University. arXiv 9 Apr 2026.

*Monthly cross-pollination pick — Month D (April): tactile sensing in prosthetics / neuroscience adjacent domain. The acoustic sensing modality for slip estimation originates in prosthetics research (Ruffini-ending-inspired shear detection via vibration) and neuromorphic/biomimetic sensing literature.*

This paper presents a parallel-jaw gripper system that uses piezoelectric microphones behind a textured silicone pad to capture contact-induced vibrations. A lightweight CNN processing log-mel spectrograms jointly predicts slip presence, direction, and magnitude — all three quantities simultaneously and continuously. Key architectural point: the multi-channel audio is treated as a 2D spectral representation, the same formulation used in speech recognition, enabling transfer of audio-domain pretraining to the tactile-slip domain.

**Cross-pollination transfer value for Sharpa + Deform Map:** The Deform Map produces deformation fields; slip is currently inferred indirectly from field dynamics. A-SLIP's formulation of *continuous slip magnitude + direction* as a joint prediction from a high-bandwidth signal is directly importable: if a lightweight acoustic or vibration sensor were co-located with each Sharpa fingertip, the log-mel spectrogram processing pipeline could run as a separate 1 kHz slip estimator feeding into the RL observation space, decoupling slip detection from the Deform Map encoder. More importantly, A-SLIP's *three-output formulation* (binary slip flag + direction + magnitude) is a clean interface for reward shaping in Q2 single-skill tasks: a slip-avoidance dense reward can be specified as `r_slip = -|magnitude| * contact_flag` without engineering contact normals or friction cone proximity.

## 🔄 Week-over-Week Synthesis

This is the first real scouting run (W16 was an example). No prior log to compare against.

The dominant theme this week is **tactile as a learned supervisory signal** rather than a raw sensor stream. All three surfaced papers converge on the same underlying tension: current visuotactile methods use tactile data as an observation to decode, but the high-bandwidth nature of tactile signals means decoding is lossy and sensor-specific. HTD (Paper 1) resolves this via latent prediction; Tac2Real (Paper 3) resolves this via a faithful sim that doesn't require perfect decoding. Paper 2 (Grounding Sim2Real) is orthogonal but provides the factorial infrastructure to measure whether any of these tactile improvements actually close the sim2real gap in practice.

A secondary theme: the field is converging on **empirical benchmarking** (Paper 2's 10k trials, A-SLIP's continuous 3-output formulation) as the ground truth for claims that were previously argued theoretically. This is a healthy sign for Q3 quantification.

**Open contradiction to flag:** Paper 1 (HTD) demonstrates near-100% success on 5 tasks using pure IL with no RL — directly challenging H2 ("near-100% on a single skill is won by reward-hacking-resistant curriculum + narrowed DR, not by automatic reward synthesis"). However, the tasks are qualitatively different from HORA's in-hand rotation benchmark (HTD uses IL with tactile on a humanoid; HORA uses RL on an isolated hand with domain randomization). The contradiction is real but the comparison is not apples-to-apples. Flag for monthly hypothesis review.
